Title: Kolkata Style Biriyani
Description: Kolkata Style Chicken Biriyani with Egg and Potato

Difficulty: Medium
Servings: 4

Cook Time: 45 Minutes

## Ingredients

### Rice & Main Ingredients
- 2 cups basmati rice
- 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
- 4 eggs
- 4 medium potatoes

### Aromatics & Vegetables
- 4 large onions, thinly sliced
- 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
- 1/4 cup mint leaves
- 1/4 cup fried onions

### Spices & Seasonings
- 1 tsp red chili powder
- 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
- 1 tsp garam masala powder
- 4-5 green cardamom
- 2 black cardamom
- 4-5 cloves
- 2 bay leaves
- 1-inch cinnamon stick
- Salt to taste

### Dairy & Liquids
- 1 cup plain yogurt
- 1/4 tsp saffron soaked in 1/4 cup warm milk
- Oil for frying

## Instructions

### Prepare Ingredients
1. Wash and soak 2 cups basmati rice for 30 minutes
   Alt: Basmati rice soaking in water
2. Cut 1 kg chicken into medium pieces and marinate with yogurt and spices
   Alt: Marinated chicken pieces in a bowl
3. Slice 4 large onions thinly and deep fry until golden brown
   Alt: Golden brown fried onions on paper towel
4. Boil 4 eggs and 4 potatoes separately until cooked
   Alt: Boiled eggs and potatoes cooling down
5. Prepare biryani masala by grinding whole spices
   Alt: Fresh ground biryani masala powder

### Cook the Dish
6. Heat oil in heavy-bottomed pot and add whole spices
   Alt: Whole spices sizzling in hot oil
7. Add marinated chicken and cook until 70% done
   Alt: Chicken cooking in the pot with spices
8. In separate pot, boil water with whole spices and salt
   Alt: Large pot of boiling spiced water
9. Add soaked rice to boiling water and cook until 70% done
   Alt: Rice cooking in boiling spiced water
10. Layer the partially cooked rice over chicken
    Alt: Rice being layered over chicken in pot

### Final Assembly and Dum Cooking
11. Sprinkle fried onions, mint leaves, and saffron soaked in milk
    Alt: Garnishing the biryani with fried onions and herbs
12. Add boiled eggs and potatoes between rice layers
    Alt: Eggs and potatoes arranged in biryani layers
13. Cover pot with aluminum foil, then place tight-fitting lid
    Alt: Pot covered with foil and lid for dum cooking
14. Cook on high heat for 3 minutes, then reduce to lowest heat for 45 minutes
    Alt: Biryani pot on low heat for dum cooking

## Notes
- Use aged basmati rice for best results
- Don't overcook the rice - it should be 70% done before layering
- Dum cooking is crucial - keep heat very low to avoid burning
- Let the biryani rest for 10 minutes before opening the lid
- Serve hot with raita and boiled eggs on the side
- Can be stored in refrigerator for up to 3 days
